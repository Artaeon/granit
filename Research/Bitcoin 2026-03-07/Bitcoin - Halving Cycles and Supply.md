## Bitcoin - Halving Cycles and Supply

The Bitcoin halving is a protocol-enforced event that cuts the block reward in half approximately every four years (every 210,000 blocks). It is the core mechanism that governs Bitcoin's monetary supply schedule and ensures its hard cap of 21 million coins.

## Supply Schedule

Bitcoin's issuance follows a predictable, disinflationary curve:

| Halving | Date | Block Height | Reward | Total BTC Issued After |
|---------|------|-------------|--------|----------------------|
| 0 (Launch) | Jan 2009 | 0 | 50 BTC | 0 |
| 1st | Nov 28, 2012 | 210,000 | 25 BTC | 10,500,000 |
| 2nd | Jul 9, 2016 | 420,000 | 12.5 BTC | 15,750,000 |
| 3rd | May 11, 2020 | 630,000 | 6.25 BTC | 18,375,000 |
| 4th | Apr 19, 2024 | 840,000 | 3.125 BTC | ~19,687,500 |
| **5th** | **~Apr 17, 2028** | **1,050,000** | **1.5625 BTC** | ~20,343,750 |

**Current status**: We are in the 4th halving epoch (April 2024 - ~April 2028), with a block reward of **3.125 BTC**.

## Key Supply Metrics

- **Maximum supply**: 21,000,000 BTC (hardcoded in the protocol)
- **Currently mined**: ~19.8 million BTC (~94.3% of total supply)
- **Over 98% mined by 2030**
- **Last bitcoin mined**: ~2140
- **Lost coins**: Estimated 3-4 million BTC permanently inaccessible (lost keys, Satoshi's coins, etc.)

## The Four-Year Cycle Theory

Historically, each halving has preceded a significant bull market:

- **2012 halving** -> 2013 bull run (BTC: $12 -> $1,100)
- **2016 halving** -> 2017 bull run (BTC: $650 -> $20,000)
- **2020 halving** -> 2021 bull run (BTC: $8,700 -> $69,000)
- **2024 halving** -> 2025 reached $126,296 ATH, but cycle broke historical pattern

## Is the Four-Year Cycle Dead?

The 2024-2025 cycle has challenged the traditional pattern. For the first time, the year following a halving (2025) finished in the red (~6% decline from January open). Wall Street analysts now argue the cycle has been disrupted:

- **Bernstein**: Predicts $150,000 in 2026
- **J.P. Morgan**: Targets ~$170,000
- **Citi Bank**: Forecasts up to $143,000

The reasoning: as Bitcoin matures into an institutional asset with ETF flows and corporate treasury adoption, the halving's supply shock becomes proportionally smaller relative to total circulating supply. [[Bitcoin - Price History and Market|Market dynamics]] are increasingly driven by demand-side factors (ETF inflows, institutional adoption) rather than supply-side halvings.

## Stock-to-Flow

Bitcoin's stock-to-flow ratio (existing supply divided by annual production) roughly doubles with each halving:

- Pre-2024: S2F ~56
- Post-2024: S2F ~112
- Post-2028: S2F ~224

For comparison, gold's S2F is ~62. After the 2024 halving, Bitcoin became harder to produce than gold by this metric.

## Why It Matters

The halving mechanism is fundamental to Bitcoin's value proposition as "digital gold":

1. **Predictable monetary policy**: Unlike fiat currencies, Bitcoin's supply schedule is known in advance and cannot be changed
2. **Decreasing inflation**: Bitcoin's annual inflation rate is currently ~0.85% and will continue falling
3. **Supply shock**: Reduced new issuance, combined with growing demand from [[Bitcoin - Regulation and Legal Status|institutional and government buyers]], creates upward price pressure
4. **Miner economics**: Each halving forces less efficient [[Bitcoin - Mining and Consensus|miners]] offline, consolidating the industry
