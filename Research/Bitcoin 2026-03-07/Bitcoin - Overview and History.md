## Bitcoin - Overview and History

Bitcoin (BTC) is the first decentralized cryptocurrency, created in 2008 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. It operates as a peer-to-peer electronic cash system without the need for intermediaries like banks or governments.

## Origins

- **2008**: Satoshi Nakamoto published the Bitcoin whitepaper, *"Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System"*
- **January 3, 2009**: The Genesis Block (Block 0) was mined, containing the embedded message: *"The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks"*
- **2010**: The first known commercial Bitcoin transaction occurred when Laszlo Hanyecz paid 10,000 BTC for two pizzas (now celebrated as "Bitcoin Pizza Day" on May 22)

## Key Properties

- **Decentralized**: No single entity controls the network; it is maintained by thousands of nodes worldwide (~24,700 reachable nodes as of 2025)
- **Fixed Supply**: Capped at 21 million BTC, enforced by the protocol's [[Bitcoin - Halving Cycles and Supply|halving schedule]]
- **Pseudonymous**: Transactions are recorded on a public ledger but linked to addresses rather than real-world identities
- **Permissionless**: Anyone can participate in the network without authorization
- **Immutable**: Once confirmed, transactions cannot be reversed or altered

## How It Works

Bitcoin uses a [[Bitcoin - How It Works|blockchain and UTXO model]] to track ownership, secured by [[Bitcoin - Mining and Consensus|proof-of-work mining]]. The network processes approximately 7 transactions per second on-chain, with the [[Bitcoin - Lightning Network and Scaling|Lightning Network]] providing additional throughput for smaller payments.

## Current State (Early 2026)

Bitcoin has matured from a niche cypherpunk experiment into a macro-level financial asset. Key metrics as of early 2026:

- **Price**: ~$67,550 (down ~46% from the October 2025 ATH of $126,296)
- **Network nodes**: ~24,700 reachable nodes
- **Merchant adoption**: Grew 74% in 2025
- **Institutional holders**: 194 public companies hold BTC on their balance sheets
- **Nation-state holders**: 23 countries now own bitcoin

The [[Bitcoin - Regulation and Legal Status|regulatory landscape]] has shifted dramatically, with the U.S. establishing a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve and multiple spot ETFs attracting over $147 billion in AUM.
