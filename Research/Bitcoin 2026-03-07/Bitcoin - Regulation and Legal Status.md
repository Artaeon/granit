## Bitcoin - Regulation and Legal Status

Bitcoin's regulatory environment has undergone a dramatic transformation between 2024 and 2026, shifting from adversarial oversight to institutional embrace, particularly in the United States.

## U.S. Strategic Bitcoin Reserve

On **March 6, 2025**, President Trump signed an executive order establishing a **Strategic Bitcoin Reserve** and a broader U.S. Digital Asset Stockpile:

- **Holdings**: The U.S. government holds an estimated **~328,372 BTC** (as of February 2026), making it the largest known state holder of bitcoin
- **Funding source**: Capitalized with crypto assets seized in criminal forfeiture proceedings
- **BITCOIN Act of 2025 (S.954)**: Introduced by Senator Cynthia Lummis, co-sponsored by 5 senators; proposes purchasing **1 million BTC** over five years using diversified federal funds. Still in committee as of March 2026
- **Implementation challenges**: Executive Director Patrick Witt acknowledged "obscure legal provisions" remain obstacles to full implementation

## State-Level Bitcoin Reserves

U.S. states are moving ahead of the federal government:

- **16 states** introduced bitcoin reserve legislation by March 2025
- **Texas** led the way, passing legislation authorizing the state comptroller to hold cryptocurrency and purchasing ~$5 million in BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT)
- **New Hampshire** and others are actively pursuing similar measures

## Spot Bitcoin ETFs

The approval of spot Bitcoin ETFs in January 2024 was a watershed moment:

- **AUM**: Over **$147 billion** in total Bitcoin ETF assets under management by early 2026, projected to reach $180-220 billion by year-end
- **BlackRock IBIT**: Dominates with ~$50 billion AUM (48.5% market share)
- **SEC actions**: Approved generic listing standards for commodity-based trust shares (October 2025), and permitted in-kind creations/redemptions for crypto ETPs
- **New ETF products**: Spot ETFs for Solana, XRP, and Litecoin also approved in late 2025
- **Distribution**: Major wirehouses including Wells Fargo, Bank of America, and Vanguard now distribute Bitcoin ETFs to clients

## GENIUS Act -- Stablecoin Regulation

On **July 18, 2025**, the GENIUS Act was signed into law, creating the first comprehensive U.S. federal framework for dollar-backed stablecoins:

- Imposed reserve requirements and audit standards
- Established clear supervisory pathways
- Signals broader regulatory acceptance of digital assets

## Global Regulatory Landscape

- **23 nation-states** now hold bitcoin (5 new in 2025)
- **El Salvador**: Continues as the first country to adopt BTC as legal tender (2021)
- **EU (MiCA)**: Markets in Crypto-Assets regulation fully in force, providing a comprehensive framework across the EU
- **China**: Maintains its ban on crypto trading and [[Bitcoin - Mining and Consensus|mining]] (though enforcement varies)

## Institutional Adoption

The regulatory clarity has accelerated institutional participation:

- **68% of institutional investors** have allocated or plan to allocate to Bitcoin ETPs
- **194 public companies** hold Bitcoin on their balance sheets
- Companies added **$54 billion** of bitcoin to their balance sheets in 2025
- In 2025, businesses, funds, ETFs, and governments bought nearly **1 million BTC**, while individuals sold 696,000 BTC -- a major shift in holder composition

## Classification

Bitcoin is classified differently across jurisdictions:

- **U.S.**: Treated as property (IRS) and a commodity (CFTC)
- **EU**: Regulated as a crypto-asset under MiCA
- **Japan**: Legal property and payment method
- **India**: Legal to hold; taxed at 30% on gains
