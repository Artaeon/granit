## Bitcoin - Price History and Market

Bitcoin's price history reflects its evolution from a worthless experiment to a globally traded macro asset with a market capitalization rivaling major companies and commodities.

## 2025 in Detail

2025 was a volatile and historically unusual year for Bitcoin:

- **January 20, 2025**: BTC surged 9% to ~$110,000 as Trump took office, promising to make the U.S. a "crypto leader"
- **April 7, 2025**: Declined to ~$74,000 (a 30%+ drop from the January highs)
- **October 6, 2025**: Reached a new all-time high of **$126,296**
- **Year-end 2025**: First time in history the post-halving year finished in the red (~6% decline from January open)

## Market Structure Changes

Bitcoin's market has fundamentally shifted with institutional participation:

### ETF Dominance
- Total Bitcoin ETF AUM: **$147 billion** (early 2026), projected $180-220 billion by year-end
- Record daily ETF inflow: **$1.38 billion** (following Trump's election)
- 2025 total ETF inflows: **$6.96 billion**
- BlackRock IBIT alone holds **$50 billion** in AUM

### Corporate Treasury Adoption
- Bitcoin treasury companies hold over **1.1 million BTC** (5.7% of supply), valued at ~$89.9 billion
- Public company adoption grew **2.5x** in 2025, with 194 companies now holding BTC
- Companies added **$54 billion** of bitcoin in 2025

### Holder Composition Shift
In 2025, a historic shift occurred:
- **Individuals sold**: 696,000 BTC
- **Institutions bought**: ~1 million BTC (businesses, funds, ETFs, governments)

This represents a transfer from retail to institutional holders, fundamentally changing Bitcoin's ownership structure.

## Grayscale's "Dawn of the Institutional Era"

Grayscale's 2026 outlook characterizes the current period as the beginning of a new institutional era, where:

- Traditional finance fully integrates Bitcoin
- Advised wealth channels open to crypto allocation
- Public blockchains bridge into mainstream financial infrastructure
- Bitcoin's correlation with traditional risk assets may evolve

## Trading Venues

- **Spot exchanges**: Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, Bitstamp
- **Derivatives**: CME (institutional), Deribit, Binance Futures
- **ETFs**: IBIT (BlackRock), FBTC (Fidelity), GBTC (Grayscale), and others
- **OTC desks**: For large institutional trades
